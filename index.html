RG
Dr. Rob Gray
Professor · Arizona State University · Perception-Action Podcast

Rob Gray is one of the most influential figures in applied skill acquisition in the world today. A Professor of Human Systems Engineering at Arizona State University, he has spent 25+ years researching how athletes learn, adapt, and perform under pressure — and translating that science into practice for coaches, physios, S&C coaches, and performance analysts at the highest levels of sport.

He has worked directly with MLB and NBA franchises and serves as Skill Acquisition Specialist with the Boston Red Sox — bringing ecological dynamics and the constraints-led approach to bear on performance problems that conventional coaching methods have failed to solve.

Rob is the author of four books on skill acquisition and coaching practice, including the widely-read How We Learn to Move and Learning to Optimise Movement — now essential reading across coaching, rehab, and sport science. His work spans practice design, return to play, athletic development, and the use of data and technology in performance environments.

His Perception-Action Podcast — launched in 2015 — has become the definitive resource for practitioners bridging research and the field, with over 500 episodes and 1 million downloads across coaching, sport science, physiotherapy, S&C, and performance analysis communities worldwide.
