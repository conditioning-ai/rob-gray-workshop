Rob Gray is one of the most influential figures in applied skill acquisition in the world today. He has spent over 25 years researching how athletes learn, adapt, and perform under pressure — and translating that science into practice at the highest levels of sport.

He has worked directly with MLB and NBA franchises and currently serves as Skill Acquisition Specialist with the Boston Red Sox — bringing ecological dynamics and the constraints-led approach to bear on performance problems that conventional coaching has failed to solve.

His podcast — the Perception-Action Podcast — is the most widely listened-to resource in the global skill acquisition community. With over 500 episodes and 1 million downloads, it is a reference point for serious practitioners across coaching, physiotherapy, S&C, and performance analysis.

What sets Rob apart from other researchers is his rare ability to make complex science immediately usable. He has a gift for stripping away jargon and presenting ideas in ways that coaches, physios, and practitioners can apply the very next session. This is not a theory-heavy academic workshop — it is a practical, hands-on working day built around implementation. People leave with tools, not just ideas.

Rob's four books — including How We Learn to Move and Learning to Optimise Movement — are now essential reading across sport science and coaching internationally. His work covers practice design, return to play, athletic development, and the application of data and video in performance environments.
